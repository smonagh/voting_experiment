The procedure on your computer screen

In each round, the first screen, shown below, informs you that you are Type A participant and that your identification number is A1.

This example screen informs you that you (A1) have two votes, A2 has two votes, and A3 has one vote. This example screen informs you that you (A1) have two votes, A2 has two votes, and A3 has one vote. The screen further informs you that the sum of the votes of all participants is 5, and that the project that receives at least 3 votes will are recommended to participant B.

The other two Type A participants are A2 and A3 will see a similar screen as you, letting them know about their subject number and their number of votes.

To move on to the next screen, click on “continue”.

After having clicked “continue“ you and the other participants in your group will see the second screen. In each round this second second screen informs you and the other two Type A participants of each of you earnings, in case Type B participant implements either Project X or Project Y. All participants of Type A receive this inforamtion at the same time, and prior to the vote.

Below is an example screen containing the earnings about Projects X and Y.

   

This screen informs you that if project X is chosen to be implemented by Type B, each of the Type A participants will earn E$16 and the Type B participant will earn E$4.

This screen further informs you that if project Y is chosen to be implemented by Type B, each of the Type A participants will earn E$4 and the Type B participant will earn E$16.

Recall, at the end of the decision making round, one of the three Type A participants will be randomly chosen to receive the earnings.

To move on to the next screen, click on “continue”.

In each round, the third screen is the decision screen. Here, you will vote on whether to send Message X or Message Y to the Type B participant.

On this decision screen, you are reminded in the upper row whether you are person A1, A2 or A3, and about the number of votes of each participant.

At the bottom of this screen you are reminded how many votes are needed for the majority rule procedure to send either Message X or Message Y to the Type B participant.

As noted previously, the Type B participant has no information about the earnings associated with either of the two projects. The Type B participant is also not informed about the procedure (total number of votes, votes assigned to each voter, that a 50% majority of votes is required for a message to be sent to Type B) 

To vote whether to send Message X or Message Y to participant B, use the computer mouse to click on that red field which indicates the message for which you would like to vote.

After you have clicked the button for either sending Message X or sending Message Y, you will advance automatically to the next round, where you and the other two Type A participants may be allocated a new identification number, (A1, A2, or A3). In this new round, you and the other two participants also may have different number of votes than in the previous round. .
